Currently I am working part time in a commercial finance team helping them out by automating their monthly reporting tasks. I have a degree in Economics and Econometrics and an honours degree in Investment Management but I have always been more interested in computers. My first real dive into programming was at the Johannesburg Stock Exchange. I joined a small team of developers whose job it was to maintain and progress an in house reporting system. Here we used T SQL and VBA to do all kinds of things ranging from data mining to ETLs. I had found my calling and wanted to learn more. So I looked into a few courses on object oriented programming. I picked one which taught Python through making games. I had just stumbled on my new favorite programming language. Since then I have used python for scripting, web development, web scraping and data munging and given that Python has become one of the defacto languages for data science it has been a perfect fit in my career at the Johannesburg Stock Exchange where data is plentiful. More recently I have begun learning client side web development through TypeScript and Google's angular framework and I am really enjoying it. In the time away from my finance job I am working on creating a software development business. The initial product is a data reporting and visualization tool which will encompass all that I have learned while working on these types of projects throughout my career.

How I keep my skills sharp
